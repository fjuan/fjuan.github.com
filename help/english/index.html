Mom's recipes

In 2007 my mom (Ana) and I started blogging about Spanish recipes at www.recetasdemama.es.

The story

I did move from Cordoba to Madrid to study and my mother wrote me a cookbook with simple and delicous recipes. As I couldn't afford to loose this book, I published it to Blogger.

Some months later, she asked me to set up a new site so she could publish new recipes but we decided to share the already existing one.

Before it had too much traction, we moved it to its own domain: recetasdemama.es

The numbers

  • Recipes: 12-15 each month since April 2007.
  • Comments: 40 new comments per recipe.
  • Visits: +350.000 uniq visitors per month.
  • Friends & Fun: Countless

The process

Ana, at Cordoba, cooks and takes pictures of everything. After enjoying it with family and friends, she selects the pictures and writes the recipe on our blog. Sometimes the recipe is recorded and Ana has to edit the video and then upload it to youtube.

The hosting

We're using a self-hosted instace of WordPress behind a lot of open source gear:

  • WordPress: one of the most popular and easy to use Content Management Systems in the world.
  • Varnish Cache: a reverse web proxy that allows to serve all our pages with a minimal cost.
  • Apache: my preferred web server. It is powerfull and has a lot of documentation.
  • MySQL: A relational database required for storing all our data.
  • Ubuntu: the server operating system (I love linux).

Books (written with my mom)

In January 2009 an editor contacted us with a comment in our blog:

Would you be interested in publishing a book with the recipes of your blog? Juan Luis Miravet

We met with JL and started working on the index of the first book. After selecting all recipes and their corresponding photos we received the first copy of our book on September 2010

It was a great success! And we started working on the second book with a very special colaboration: Rafael de Juan, my brother.

While the first book has all kinds of recipes, the second one is focused on desserts. Be carefull with the temptation!
